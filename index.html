What this measures

Rapid gray letters flash on screen. A red digit flashes among gray letters. You must identify it (Even or Odd?) — this captures your attention and resets theta phase. Shortly after, a gray X may appear. Answer both questions after each trial. Only trials where you correctly identified the digit count toward your theta estimate — ensuring the blink is real.

G
Gray letters flash rapidly one at a time. Ignore them completely. Keep your eyes on the center.
O
Red O appears occasionally. Your brain grabs it automatically — you don't need to do anything.
X
Gray X is your only target. After each trial, answer Yes or No — did you see it? It is the same color as all other letters. Identify it by shape.
Why gray X? A colored X would "pop out" visually and bypass the attentional bottleneck we're trying to measure. A gray X forces your brain to use temporal shape discrimination — the process governed by the theta rhythm.
